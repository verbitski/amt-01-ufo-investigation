# Privacy

This notice describes the privacy boundary of the AMT-01 Open Investigation, including its consent-based private witness-intake form.

## The Project Website

The project website is a static site. It does not operate user accounts, private messaging, mailing lists, advertising trackers, or project-set cookies. Its private witness-intake form is hosted separately by Tally and opens as an external service.

The website uses [Vercel Web Analytics](https://vercel.com/docs/analytics) to understand aggregate readership, including page views and general traffic patterns. The project does not configure custom events, user profiles, cross-site tracking, advertising audiences, or analytics on the separate Tally form.

Vercel states that Web Analytics does not use cookies and stores anonymized, aggregated data rather than information associated with an individual or IP address. Depending on the request, analytics dimensions may include the time, visited URL or path, filtered query parameters, referrer, coarse country or region, operating system, browser, device type, and analytics-script version. Vercel derives a visitor identifier from the incoming request and discards that identifier after 24 hours, preventing recognition across different days or websites. See [Vercel's Web Analytics privacy documentation](https://vercel.com/docs/analytics/privacy-policy).

The purpose is to learn whether people find and read the public project material and which public pages or referral sources are useful. Analytics data is available to the project steward in the Vercel dashboard and is not sold or used by the project for advertising. The current Hobby-plan dashboard provides a one-month reporting window; Vercel states that it may retain data longer than the guaranteed reporting window. See [Vercel's limits and pricing documentation](https://vercel.com/docs/analytics/limits-and-pricing).

Do not put personal or sensitive information in a website URL or query string. Browser privacy tools and content blockers may prevent analytics requests. If the analytics provider, collected dimensions, event configuration, purpose, access, or retention terms change materially, this notice must be reviewed before or when the change takes effect.

Separately from Web Analytics, the hosting provider may process technical data needed to deliver and protect the site, such as an IP address, request time, requested URL, browser or device information, and security logs. That infrastructure processing is controlled by the hosting provider and its own privacy terms, not by a project-managed database.

## Private Witness Intake and Tally

The project accepts private witness reports only through the [AMT-01 witness-intake form](https://tally.so/r/5BgZMb). Do not send a raw report through GitHub, Discord, email, or an unsolicited direct message.

The data controller is the individual AMT-01 project operator associated with the public GitHub account [@verbitski](https://github.com/verbitski). The durable private contact is [werbitsky@gmail.com](mailto:werbitsky@gmail.com). The Tally form is the primary rights-request route; the contact email may also be used for a privacy request. If both routes are unavailable, a project Discord moderator may privately route a request, but Discord is not an evidence-storage channel.

[Tally](https://tally.so/) hosts the form and processes and stores responses on the project's behalf. Tally describes itself as the data processor for respondent data in its [GDPR information](https://tally.so/help/gdpr) and operates its own infrastructure, security controls, technical logs, cookies, and service practices under its [privacy notice](https://tally.so/help/privacy-policy).

At the project level, access to raw form responses is limited to the project steward acting as the private-intake curator. Tally and its authorized service providers may process the data as needed to operate the form and response service. The project does not claim that an Internet form is risk-free or appropriate for emergency, legally privileged, classified, or highly sensitive material.

Depending on the route and answers selected, the form may collect:

- confirmation that the submitter is at least 18 and agrees to the stated private-review terms;
- a general event location and approximate date or time;
- a narrative of what happened and descriptions of appearance, movement, duration, sounds, environmental conditions, effects, other witnesses, and any media captured;
- distinctions between direct memory and later estimates, plus a confidence indication;
- optional name or pseudonym and optional contact email;
- an optional private reference phrase to help locate a later privacy request;
- optional files, limited to five files of 10 MB each and restricted to images, video, audio, PDF, and plain text, together with rights and use-permission choices; and
- access, copy, correction, consent-withdrawal, deletion, or other privacy-request details when that route is selected.

The information is collected to review and organize a report, distinguish recollection from estimate, assess whether follow-up would be useful, manage consent and rights, and respond to privacy requests. Submission does not authenticate an event and does not guarantee inclusion in a dataset or case record.

Where the GDPR or equivalent consent rules apply, the project relies on the submitter's affirmative consent for voluntary private intake and review. Consent may be withdrawn at any time through the privacy-request route. Withdrawal does not make earlier consent-based processing unlawful, and a minimal record may still be kept where required to document the request, comply with law, or establish or defend legal claims.

The form uses Tally's Google reCAPTCHA integration to reduce automated abuse. Loading or completing it may cause Tally and Google to process technical information such as IP address, browser or device data, cookies, and interaction signals under their own terms. See [Tally's reCAPTCHA documentation](https://tally.so/help/recaptcha) and [Google's privacy policy](https://policies.google.com/privacy). The project does not make decisions producing legal or similarly significant effects solely by automated means.

## Data Minimization

Provide only what is needed for research review. Use a general public-area description rather than a home address, current private location, precise coordinates, lodging details, workplace, or another location that could identify or endanger a person. Name and contact fields are optional. Do not submit identity documents, medical records, credentials, confidential employer material, classified information, or personal information about another person.

Files are optional and may contain hidden metadata such as coordinates, device identifiers, account names, or timestamps. Remove unnecessary metadata before uploading. The launch intake does not accept a recognizable face, voice, vehicle identifier, or other third-party personal data. Do not upload third-party work unless you have the authority needed for private review.

## Private Review Is Not Publication Consent

A Tally submission is private by default and is not automatically published, added to GitHub, posted to Discord, licensed under the repository's public licenses, or treated as evidence supporting AMT-01. The form's storage-and-review consent authorizes private intake review only.

If the project wants to quote, publish, license, or add any part of a submission or uploaded file to a public case record or dataset, it must obtain separate, specific publication permission and agree the scope, attribution or de-identification choice, redaction, and applicable license with the submitter. A form answer allowing follow-up or research use does not remove this separate-publication requirement.

## Access and Retention

Every raw Tally submission will be reviewed and manually deleted from the active response list no later than 12 months after submission. This applies whether or not the report appears useful for later case-record work. The current Tally workspace does not provide the Business-plan automatic retention control, so this is a manual project process rather than a platform-enforced expiry. The curator may delete irrelevant, unsafe, duplicate, or withdrawn material sooner.

Under Tally's [deletion process](https://tally.so/help/how-to-delete-and-recover-form-data), a deleted response moves to Trash and may remain recoverable there for up to 90 days unless Trash is emptied earlier. Tally states that permanently deleted form data is cleared from its backups within 90 days. These provider-controlled intervals mean project deletion from the active response list may occur before final removal from every Tally recovery layer.

Before the 12-month limit expires, the submitter and curator may separately agree that identified material will be copied into a de-identified case record or retained source record for a stated purpose and period. That agreement must identify the exact material, access boundary, retention basis, publication status, and withdrawal or deletion consequences. Acceptance for further review does not create that agreement and does not authorize public disclosure. The original raw Tally response remains subject to the 12-month active-list limit.

## Privacy Rights and Requests

Use the privacy-request route in the [same intake form](https://tally.so/r/5BgZMb). Depending on applicable law, a submitter may request access, a copy, correction, deletion, restriction, portability, or withdrawal of consent, and may object to some processing. Include the private reference phrase or original contact email if available, but provide only the information needed to locate the submission and assess the request. A response email is required so the curator can verify the requester and communicate the outcome. The project may seek other reasonable verification before changing or deleting a record, especially where a request could affect another person or a rights record.

Requests will be handled as soon as practicable, with a target of responding or completing the initial assessment within 30 days. This is an operational target, not a guaranteed deadline or a limitation on rights available under applicable law. Complex verification, safety, platform, backup, or legal issues may take longer. Limits for already public or independently copied material are described in [CONSENT_AND_TAKEDOWN.md](CONSENT_AND_TAKEDOWN.md).

If applicable privacy law gives the requester the right to complain to a supervisory authority, the requester may contact the authority for the place where they live or work or where the alleged issue occurred. This project notice does not limit that right.

Tally, Google reCAPTCHA, hosting, and other service providers may process data in countries or through subprocessors described in their own notices and contractual safeguards. The project does not intentionally disclose private witness material to unrelated third parties, sell it, or use it for advertising.

## GitHub and Discord

The project uses GitHub for the public repository and Discord for public community discussion. GitHub and Discord operate their own accounts, infrastructure, logs, cookies, moderation systems, and privacy practices. Review their terms and privacy notices before using those services.

GitHub issues, pull requests, commits, review comments, and other public repository activity are public. Discord channels identified as public should also be treated as public. Public content may be indexed, quoted, copied, archived, mirrored, or included in repository history.

A private direct message to a Discord moderator is not visible in a public channel, but it is still processed by Discord. Use moderator direct messages only for conduct, privacy, takedown, or security concerns, and share the minimum information necessary.

## Do Not Post Sensitive Material

Do not post raw witness reports or identifying information in public GitHub or Discord spaces. This includes:

- names and private contact details;
- exact private locations or details that reveal a person's home, workplace, or lodging;
- faces, voices, vehicle identifiers, or personal documents;
- original files containing location, device, account, or other sensitive metadata;
- medical, employment, immigration, financial, or similarly sensitive information;
- information about another person that you do not have permission to share.

Before making a public contribution, remove information that is not necessary for the research purpose and check files for hidden metadata. A public username, profile, commit author name, or commit email may also reveal your identity.

## Repository History and Copies

Material intentionally submitted to a public Git repository can remain in commit history, forks, clones, mirrors, caches, release archives, or copies held by other people. The project can correct or remove material from locations it controls, but it cannot guarantee deletion from independent copies.

## Privacy Concerns

For access, a copy, correction, consent withdrawal, deletion, or another privacy concern involving a private witness submission, use the privacy-request route in the [witness-intake form](https://tally.so/r/5BgZMb) or email [werbitsky@gmail.com](mailto:werbitsky@gmail.com). If both routes are unavailable, privately contact a moderator in the [project Discord server](https://discord.gg/JVvHf5cXhs) and share only the minimum needed to route the request. For sensitive conduct or security triage unrelated to a witness submission, use the same moderator route.

Requests are handled under [CONSENT_AND_TAKEDOWN.md](CONSENT_AND_TAKEDOWN.md). Platform-level requests concerning GitHub, Discord, Tally, or the website hosting provider may also need to be made directly to that provider.
