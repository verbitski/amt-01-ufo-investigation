Witness-led UFO investigationSummer 2004 · Antalya coast

I witnessed something I still cannot explain.

Near the Antalya coast, I saw a dark triangular object move past at close range and low altitude. I did not record it, and I do not claim to know what it was. AMT-01 is my framework for documenting the experience, reverse-engineering the reported constraints, and curating ideas that can be compared with what I remember.

This is a witness-led project, not a predetermined conclusion. Read what I saw, challenge the method, or help test an idea.

The encounter

01 / The encounter

What I remember seeing.

In summer 2004, I recall a dark, matte triangular form moving slowly past at close range. I later estimated that it was perhaps 15–20 metres away laterally and about 4–5 metres above the local ground or water. It remained in view for roughly 12–15 seconds. Its flat-looking underside carried several sharply bounded, irregular regions that looked completely black, with no reflection visible to me. I remember no obvious sound or visible means of propulsion.

Detailed appearance

What the object looked like to me.

This is a retrospective memory, not a measurement. “Not noticed” does not prove physical absence.

I remember an elongated, flat-looking triangular underside with several irregular, completely black regions. It appeared to move steadily, with no obvious sound, lights, exhaust, or downwash. Its exact shape, size, material, and mechanism remain unknown.

Full object description
RememberedOverall form
An elongated triangular or arrow-like outline with a sharp, blade-like nose and a straight rear edge. The nose remained aligned with the direction of travel.
RememberedVisible side
Only the underside and outer contour were clearly visible. The upper geometry and thickness were not observed. A later impression suggested a nearly flat or low-profile body, possibly up to roughly one metre high. This impression is explicitly uncertain.
RememberedUnderside surface
The underside appeared flat, continuous, and seamless, without visible panels, joints, hatches, or mechanical segmentation. Under the lamps it looked dark, matte, and metal-like, similar in appearance to dark anodized aluminium. Its actual material is unknown.
Remembered + estimateUnusually dark regions
Several sharply bounded, irregular areas appeared much darker than the surrounding underside. I remember them as completely black, with no internal reflection visible to me. They looked light-absorbing, but the observation cannot establish what they were made of or how they interacted with light. I later estimated roughly 5–7, concentrated around the middle and rear. Their exact count, shape, size, orientation, and placement remain uncertain.
Rough estimateApparent size
Approximately 6–8 metres long and 2.5–3 metres wide at the rear. These are later visual estimates, not measurements or a site-calibrated reconstruction.
RememberedOrientation and motion
It appeared to move nose-first from right to left, parallel to the coast, along a straight and level path. I noticed no obvious climb, descent, banking, wobble, course correction, acceleration, or deceleration. I later estimated a visible path of 40–50 metres, corresponding to roughly 10–15 km/h over the recalled interval. Neither value was measured.
Not noticedPropulsion and effects
I noticed no obvious rotors, nozzles, exhaust, navigation lights, glow, or other visible means of propulsion. I also noticed no sound or obvious environmental effects such as downwash, heat, pressure, unusual odour, lamp disturbance, or movement of nearby material.
RememberedVisibility and fading
It became visible when its underside entered weak light from the walkway lamps. After the lamp row ended, a faint contour remained for roughly one second and then became too dark for me to see. I did not observe a sudden or unusual disappearance.
UnknownWhat remains unresolved
The complete three-dimensional shape, thickness, mass, construction, internal volume, and the nature of the darker regions remain unknown, as do any support mechanism, energy source, operator, or origin.

02 / What it can establish

What this witness record can and cannot establish.

I use “UFO” because I have not identified what I saw. It describes that uncertainty. It is not a claim about extraterrestrial origin or extraordinary technology.

  • 01
    I can document what I remember

    The case preserves my witness record and its later corrections. It does not independently prove what caused the experience.

  • 02
    Memory is not measurement

    Time, height, size, distance, and speed were not instrumentally measured and may change under different assumptions.

  • 03
    Several explanations remain possible

    Environmental, perceptual, conventional, and less familiar possibilities should be tested against the same information.

?

My current conclusion: I still do not know what it was. The honest result may remain uncertain unless new evidence becomes available.

03 / The framework

The reverse-engineering framework I want to build.

I want to curate an open process that preserves what I experienced, marks uncertainty clearly, and compares several explanations, including ordinary ones, under the same rules.

  1. 01

    Preserve the experience

    My wording, corrections, and uncertainties stay versioned. A remembered detail remains a report, not a measurement.

    ≠
  2. 02

    Turn recollections into testable constraints

    Estimates and calculations must reveal their assumptions so anyone can challenge them or try a different range.

    ±
  3. 03

    Compare ideas under the same rules

    No explanation receives special treatment. Each must be compared with the same witness record, uncertainty, and missing information.

    ⇄
