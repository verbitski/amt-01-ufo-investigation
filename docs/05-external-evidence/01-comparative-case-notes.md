# Comparative Case Notes

These are provisional comparison targets, not proof that AMT-01 shares a common origin or mechanism. Inclusion here records a lead for source review. It does not authenticate the report.

## C-01 — DOW-UAP-D54, Mediterranean Sea

**Match type:** morphology only.

Reported in modern mission-report material as a **triangular and metallic** UAP with recorded altitude/speed fields.

Relevance:

- official operational-report context;
- triangular/metallic description;
- Mediterranean region.

Mismatch:

- much higher altitude;
- conventional aircraft-like speed range;
- no AMT-01-level data on silence, flat underside, black windows, or downwash.

Classification: **shape match, not physics match**.

Review status: **original PDF checked at page 7; broader context review still required**.

Primary document:

- https://www.war.gov/medialink/ufo/release_1/dow-uap-d54-mission-report-mediterranean-sea-na.pdf

## C-02 — Boeing Field dark triangular object, 1947

Reported as dark/blue triangular and slow, but fluttering like a leaf; multiple engineers compared it to tar paper.

Relevance:

- triangle, dark color, slow motion.

Mismatch:

- lightweight flutter;
- wind-borne behavior;
- no rigid stable trajectory.

Classification: **negative control** demonstrating that shape/color/speed alone are insufficient.

Original source and locator:

- https://www.war.gov/medialink/ufo/release_1/65_hs1-834228961_62-hq-83894_section_3.pdf, PDF page 178

## C-03 — Bethel, Alaska, 1947

Pilots reported a smooth, streamlined object larger than their DC-3, with no visible means of propulsion, at roughly 1,000 ft.

Relevance:

- trained aviation observers;
- smooth surface;
- no visible propulsion;
- apparently high performance.

Mismatch:

- different shape;
- no close-range surface detail;
- speed/range estimates remain witness-based.

Original source and locator:

- https://www.war.gov/medialink/ufo/release_1/65_hs1-834228961_62-hq-83894_section_2.pdf, PDF page 183

Discovery transcription, not authoritative:

## C-04 — Hamilton Field / P-80 comparison, 1947

Two white shiny objects were reported moving such that a P-80 appeared nearly motionless by comparison; no vapor, smoke, or visible trail was reported. Sound could not be assessed because of the P-80.

Relevance:

- apparent high speed;
- no trail.

Mismatch:

- circular/shiny morphology;
- no acoustic constraint;
- distance and speed not instrumentally established.

Original source and locator:

- https://www.war.gov/medialink/ufo/release_1/65_hs1-834228961_62-hq-83894_section_2.pdf, PDF page 186

## C-05 — Montana rapid departure, 1947

A disc was reported hovering/fluttering and then departing at “tremendous speed,” disappearing in clear air rather than behind an obstruction.

Relevance:

- transition from low-speed state to rapid departure;
- reported absence of ordinary occlusion.

Mismatch:

- purely visual speed estimate;
- disc morphology;
- no sensor data.

Original source and locator:

## Comparative conclusion

This small, non-systematic set demonstrates why superficial descriptor matching is insufficient. It does not establish how common any AMT-01 feature is and cannot support a conclusion that no other case has a similar feature combination. A defensible comparison requires a frozen corpus, recorded search strategy, screened-out cases, duplicates, and negative controls.

```text
triangular
+ close range
+ low altitude
+ rigid slow motion
+ no sound
+ no downwash
+ flat seamless underside
+ irregular regions remembered as completely black
```
