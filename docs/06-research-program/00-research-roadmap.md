# Research Roadmap

This is a proposed research program. Completion of a documentation phase does not validate the witness account or any model. Force Skin, MEVE, and Q-MEVE remain conceptual tools whose physical realizability has not been established.

## Phase 0 — Knowledge consolidation

**Status:** initial consolidation completed in v1.0; public consistency and provenance review continues.

Outputs:

- structured documentation;
- claim ledger;
- hypothesis registry;
- model and hypothesis inventory;
- source and evidence protocol.

## Phase 1 — Formal low-speed model

Objective:

Turn Force Skin from a narrative into a controlled mathematical black box.

Outputs:

- body geometry and coordinate system;
- mass scenarios;
- force-density distributions;
- stability/control model;
- wind-disturbance rejection model;
- minimum observable signatures for airflow-based alternatives.

## Phase 2 — Boundary physics

Objective:

Formalize the conditional requirements of the MEVE/Q-MEVE conceptual sandbox without presenting it as established physics.

Outputs:

- exclusion-region geometry;
- external pressure stress;
- precursor length/time requirements;
- momentum-transfer accounting;
- wake restoration;
- energy lower bounds;
- identified impossibility conditions.

## Phase 3 — Sensor-window architecture

Objective:

Compare the aperture hypothesis with ordinary surface, lighting, perceptual, and memory explanations for the reported black appearance.

Outputs:

- visible/IR transmission-reflection models;
- candidate material classes;
- sensor coverage layouts;
- low-observable tradeoffs;
- sealed transmedium-window requirements.

## Phase 4 — High-speed/transmedium scaling

Objective:

Quantify where Force Skin becomes insufficient and full boundary control is required.

Outputs:

- air-density/altitude maps;
- shock and heating bounds;
- underwater pressure/cavitation bounds;
- transition conditions;
- expected observables for imperfect envelopes.

## Phase 5 — Comparative corpus analysis

Objective:

Search official records for cases with overlapping physical signatures, not merely “UFO” terminology.

Outputs:

- ranked case matrix;
- source verification links;
- morphology and signature clusters;
- negative controls;
- contradiction analysis.

## Phase 6 — Safe experiments and simulations

Objective:

Test optical and aerodynamic bounds without attempting exotic propulsion.

Outputs:

- black-window optical mockups;
- visibility simulations;
- CFD or actuator-disk upper bounds;
- camera geometry reconstruction;
- low-risk sensor/material experiments.
