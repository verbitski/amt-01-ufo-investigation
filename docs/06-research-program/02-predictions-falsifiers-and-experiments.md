# Predictions, Falsifiers, and Experiments

The model predictions below are conditional. They are not predictions already confirmed by AMT-01, and none of the named project models is an established physical explanation.

## 1. Force Skin predictions

If support is continuously distributed:

- attitude motion should be smoother than a comparable multirotor;
- control corrections may appear as no visible body tilt;
- force resultant should track the center of mass;
- black apertures need not be symmetric if they are sensors rather than primary thrusters.

### Falsifier

A demonstrated conventional or perceptual account that reproduces the retained reported features under plausible conditions would remove the modeling motivation for Force Skin. The object's mass is unknown, and distance, height, speed, and size are later estimates.

## 2. Sensor-window predictions

If the reported black regions were spectral windows:

- visible reflectance can approach zero while transmission remains high in another band;
- geometry may follow sensor coverage rather than structural symmetry;
- the regions should be sealed and flush;
- their apparent blackness may vary strongly with wavelength and angle.

### Safe experiment

Build non-flight optical mockups to test whether ordinary materials can produce the remembered appearance using:

- matte dark body panels;
- ordinary glass;
- anti-reflection-coated optical elements;
- visible-blocking/IR-transmitting filters;
- deep baffles/light traps.

Photograph under low lamp illumination across several plausible geometries and compare the perceived dark-region effect. This can test visual plausibility, not identify the original cause.

## 3. Airflow-model falsification test

Use actuator-disk and CFD simulations to determine ground-level flow under mass scenarios. Compare against human-detection thresholds for:

- grass movement;
- loose sand;
- clothing/hair;
- microphone levels.

No high-energy hardware is required.

## 4. MEVE predictions

Within the MEVE concept, a matter-exclusion boundary would need to reduce direct surface interaction and may imply observable effects at an outer boundary:

- pressure gradients;
- condensation or refractive distortion;
- plasma/ionization if electromagnetic;
- displaced dust or water;
- delayed wake closure;
- electromagnetic emissions.

### Falsifier

If the reported low-interaction signatures are adequately explained by memory or scale error, sensor artifacts, or conventional fluid dynamics, MEVE loses its modeling motivation.

## 5. Q-MEVE predictions

Within the highly speculative Q-MEVE abstraction, an imperfect Level-3 boundary might imply:

- precursor optical distortion;
- abrupt density gradients;
- unusual phase/condensation behavior;
- nonlocal or broadly distributed pressure response;
- suppressed direct wake with a wider weak disturbance.

### Falsifier

A physically complete proposal must conserve momentum and stress. A version requiring matter or momentum to vanish without an accounting path is not admissible within the project.

## 6. High-speed/transmedium predictions

For a real low-altitude hypersonic or underwater event, conventional physics predicts strong shock, sound, heat, plasma, cavitation, and wake. A claimed absence is meaningful only if:

- distance and speed are independently constrained;
- sensor bandwidth and saturation are understood;
- audio timing is preserved;
- the path intersects a medium where interaction should be observable.

## 7. Safety boundary

Current project experiments are limited to:

- optics;
- passive sensors;
- geometry;
- software simulation;
- low-power bench measurements.

High-voltage, high-field, plasma, radiation, pressure-vessel, cryogenic, or energetic experiments require qualified facilities and are outside the present project scope.
