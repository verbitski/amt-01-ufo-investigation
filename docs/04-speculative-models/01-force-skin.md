# Force Skin Model

> **Conceptual sandbox:** This document explores a conditional model. It is not an observation, recovered technology, established physics, or evidence of what caused the reported AMT-01 event.

## Definition

Force Skin is a project-defined requirements abstraction intended to represent the reported low-speed motion without selecting a physical mechanism.

> In the model, a hypothetical continuous or quasi-continuous surface/volume interface would produce distributed support, stabilization, and vector control without conventional discrete thrusters.

## Mathematical black box

Let the underside occupy surface \(S\), with force density:

\[\mathbf{f}(x,y,t)\]

Total force:

\[\mathbf{F}=\int_S \mathbf{f}(x,y,t)\,dA\]

Hover condition:

\[F_z\approx mg\]

Moment balance about the center of mass:

\[\int_S \mathbf{r}\times\mathbf{f}\,dA\approx0\]

A simple control distribution:

\[p(x,y,t)=p_0+a(t)+b(t)(x-x_{cm})+c(t)y\]

where:

- \(p_0\): baseline support;
- \(a(t)\): altitude command;
- \(b(t)\): pitch gradient;
- \(c(t)\): roll gradient.

Yaw and horizontal translation may be generated by lateral components, asymmetric boundary conditions, or a small vector tilt.

## Reported motion the model is designed to represent

If such an interface existed, the abstraction could represent the witness's recalled motion through these modeled properties:

- force spread rather than point-applied;
- small, continuous attitude corrections;
- no requirement for rotor-speed oscillations;
- corrections that might not be visually apparent;
- motion that could appear rigid and inertial;
- wind compensation through small lateral force changes.

## Role of the black regions

Conditional roles considered within the model:

1. sensor windows measuring altitude, terrain, motion, pressure, or field state;
2. field-control apertures modifying local force density;
3. optical traps hiding internal optics;
4. diagnostic nodes for closed-loop control.

If the remembered layout is approximately correct, assigning all modeled support force to those regions alone could create large moments unless another balancing layer were also assumed.

## Unknown coupling

Force Skin specifies a hypothetical force distribution but does not identify a real interaction or reaction channel. Conditional reservoirs considered for bookkeeping include:

- surrounding medium through an unknown distributed mechanism;
- an electromagnetic or gravitational field;
- Earth or a larger inertial system;
- a more general field stress;
- an unknown interaction.

Any physical implementation would still have to conserve momentum.
