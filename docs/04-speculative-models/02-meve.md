# MEVE — Matter-Exclusion Vacuum Envelope

> **Conceptual sandbox:** This document explores a conditional model. It is not an observation, recovered technology, established physics, or evidence of what caused the reported AMT-01 event.

## Definition

MEVE is a project-defined extension that asks what environmental isolation would require if a Force Skin-like support model were assumed.

```text
external medium
    ↓
active pressure-bearing boundary
    ↓
near-vacuum / low-density exclusion region
    ↓
physical hull
```

In this concept, the inner gap could be vacuum or near-vacuum. The unexplained element would be a moving boundary capable of limiting matter ingress while carrying pressure stress.

## Why an air bubble is insufficient

A gas bubble is medium-specific and still produces a hydrodynamic/aerodynamic obstacle. It does not generalize cleanly to water, other atmospheres, dust, plasma, and space.

A hypothetical platform intended to work across different media would require:

> a boundary that regulates coupling to whatever matter surrounds it.

## Why a passive vacuum bubble is insufficient

External air or water would still strike the outer boundary, producing:

- drag;
- shock;
- heat;
- wake;
- cavitation in water;
- acoustic disturbance.

Any MEVE-like proposal would therefore need an active interface rather than only a hard invisible shell.

## Functional architecture

### Near-hull exclusion zone

Would aim to prevent direct wetting, friction, and impact at the material hull.

### Boundary stress shell

Would have to carry external pressure. At sea level, a vacuum boundary would experience approximately 101 kPa pressure difference. At depth, water pressure rises approximately as:

\[p=p_{atm}+\rho gd\]

### Precursor region

Would act ahead of a hypothetical platform to alter medium conditions before contact.

### Wake-restoration region

Would return the medium behind the hypothetical platform toward its normal state while minimizing turbulence and abrupt collapse.

## Pressure problem

For scale, a hypothetical 10 m² projected area under 101 kPa pressure corresponds to order-meganewton symmetric boundary stress. Symmetry could yield low net force, but any proposed boundary would still have to carry the local stress.

## Transmedium interpretation

Within the model, air and water modes would become variations of one boundary-control problem:

| Environment | Required function |
|---|---|
| Low-speed air | Support and wind rejection |
| High-speed air | Shock, heat, and compression management |
| Low-speed water | Wetting, drag, wake, and acoustic control |
| High-speed water | Pressure, shock, and cavitation exclusion |
| Space | Envelope may collapse to navigation/inertial functions |
