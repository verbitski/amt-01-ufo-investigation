# Control, Autonomy, and Communications

> **Conceptual sandbox:** This document explores a conditional architecture. It is not an observation, recovered technology, established physics, or evidence of what caused the reported AMT-01 event.

## 1. Control hierarchy

If a Force Skin- or MEVE-like system existed, implementing it would likely require multiple timescales of control.

### Level 0 — Field stabilization

- maintains support or exclusion boundary;
- compensates rapid local disturbances;
- likely operates as deterministic control loops rather than deliberative AI;
- may require microsecond-to-millisecond response.

### Level 1 — Vehicle dynamics

- altitude;
- roll, pitch, yaw;
- velocity vector;
- wind rejection;
- transition among operating modes.

### Level 2 — Navigation and perception

- terrain and obstacle sensing;
- localization;
- route following;
- sensor fusion;
- environmental prediction.

### Level 3 — Mission autonomy

- task planning;
- observation targets;
- stealth policy;
- abort/return behavior;
- interaction rules.

### Level 4 — Strategic tasking

- remote operator;
- carrier platform;
- distributed network;
- autonomous mission authority;
- unknown command source.

## 2. AI role

Within this hypothetical architecture, mission-level AI could be useful because communication delays, high-speed dynamics, and environmental complexity could make continuous manual control inefficient.

AI would not replace lower-level physics/control loops. One hypothetical architecture is:

```text
fast feedback controllers
+ state estimation
+ model-predictive control
+ perception/navigation AI
+ mission planner
```

## 3. AMT-01 behavior

The witness recalls no visible reaction to their presence and remembers a steady path. Many explanations could produce that appearance; it does not establish autonomy, a preplanned trajectory, or intelligence.

## 4. Quantum communication

Known quantum entanglement does not permit controllable faster-than-light message transmission. Therefore it cannot serve as a known instantaneous remote-control channel.

In a hypothetical system, known or future quantum technologies might contribute to:

- sensing;
- clocks and synchronization;
- inertial navigation;
- cryptography;
- field-state measurement;
- materials and control hardware.

## 5. Conditional communication alternatives

- conventional radio/laser communication when geometry permits;
- burst communication with a local carrier;
- preloaded mission plans;
- delayed updates;
- fully autonomous operation;
- a still-unknown physical channel.
