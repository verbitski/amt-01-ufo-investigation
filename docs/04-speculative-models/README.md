# Speculative Models

> **Conceptual sandbox:** These documents explore conditional models. They are not observations, recovered technology, established physics, or evidence of what caused the reported AMT-01 event.

This directory is intentionally separated from the AMT-01 witness record, estimates, and derived calculations.

Force Skin, MEVE, Q-MEVE, transmedium, control, and warp-related documents are project-developed thought models. They are not observations, recovered technologies, established physics, or evidence of the object's identity or origin.

## How to read these documents

- **Force Skin** is treated as a mechanism-neutral distributed-support requirements model. It describes a force and control problem without identifying a reaction channel.
- **MEVE** explores what complete local medium-interaction management would require. It is not established by AMT-01.
- **Q-MEVE** is a highly speculative exclusion-boundary abstraction with no known demonstrated macroscopic mechanism.
- **High-speed, transmedium, and warp material** extends far beyond the behavior reported in AMT-01. The witness record contains no high-speed, underwater, inertial, or interstellar observation.
- **Control and autonomy material** is conditional architecture built on speculative system assumptions.

An accepted project decision to explore or retain a model means only that the model remains organized for analysis. It does not increase the evidence for that model.

## Review standard

A useful model review should state:

1. which recorded claims or later estimates it addresses;
2. which inputs and assumptions it requires;
3. how momentum, energy, pressure, and entropy are accounted for;
4. what observable signatures it predicts;
5. what evidence would strengthen, weaken, or falsify it;
6. whether a simpler conventional or perceptual explanation remains viable.
