# AMT-01 Full Observation Record

This is the current structured witness record. It describes what the witness remembers and clearly marks later estimates. It is not a contemporaneous statement or instrumented reconstruction. Earlier project values remain visible in the [memory and correction timeline](04-memory-and-correction-timeline.md).

## 1. Scene

- Mediterranean coast of Turkey, somewhere near Antalya.
- Boundary between a beach and hotel territory.
- Pedestrian path running along the beach.
- Round lamps installed near the boundary between the hotel-side landscaping/path and beach-side area.
- Summer 2004; exact day and month unknown.
- Warm night, approximately 21:00–22:00.
- Light waves on the shore.
- Quiet environment; conversation with friends was comfortable without raised voices.
- Relatively few people nearby.
- Wind direction, corrected later: **from the shore toward the sea**.

## 2. Witness position

The witness stood closer to the hotel territory than to the water. Friends were nearby but occupied in conversation and did not look in time despite being called.

## 3. Appearance and acquisition

The witness was already facing the direction from which the object entered the illuminated part of the scene and noticed its motion immediately. The object became visible as its underside entered the upward/scattered illumination from the lamps.

The object remained against the night sky. The lamps stayed fully visible and were not occluded. The object was slightly offset from the lamp line and appeared somewhat closer to the witness than the lamps' exact vertical line.

## 4. Geometry

- Elongated isosceles triangular/arrow planform.
- Sharp, blade-like nose.
- Nose aligned with direction of travel.
- Straight rear edge.
- Later approximate length estimate: 6–8 m.
- Later approximate rear-width estimate: 2.5–3 m.
- Underside appeared strictly flat.
- No visible seams, panels, joints, hatches, or assembly lines.
- Only underside and contour were clearly visible.
- Upper body shape was not visible.
- Thickness could not be established.
- A late visual impression suggested either a nearly flat body or a low-profile/pyramidal form possibly up to roughly 1 m high, but this is explicitly uncertain.

## 5. Surface

- Main surface visually resembled dark anodized aluminum.
- Matte rather than glossy.
- No sharp specular reflections.
- Illumination appeared diffuse and relatively even.
- Contour remained geometrically sharp across the full object.

## 6. Black regions

- Later count estimate: approximately 5–7 regions.
- Irregular polygonal or torn-looking shapes.
- Not circular or oval.
- Apparently similar in scale, although this is not certain.
- No obvious bilateral symmetry.
- Sharp boundaries.
- Appeared completely black to the witness compared with the surrounding underside.
- No internal reflection was visible.
- It was impossible to determine whether these were physical openings, recessed optical traps, windows, or surface materials.

## 7. Motion

- Nose-forward.
- Parallel to the coastline/path.
- Right-to-left from the observer's viewpoint.
- Roughly perpendicular to the shore-to-sea wind.
- Appeared level at the available visual resolution.
- No obvious climb, descent, pitch, roll, yaw, flutter, or oscillation was noticed.
- No course correction was noticed.
- No obvious acceleration or deceleration was noticed.
- Approximate path in view: 40–50 m.
- Approximate duration: 12–15 s.

## 8. Proximity and altitude

- Lamp height estimated at 3–3.5 m.
- Object estimated at 4–5 m altitude.
- Closest lateral distance from witness to the object's path later estimated at 15–20 m.
- The object felt close enough that a person could have attempted to run alongside or throw an object toward it.

## 9. Environmental signatures

The witness does not remember noticing any of the following under the recalled conditions. These are uncalibrated negative observations, not proof of physical zero:

- engine or rotor noise;
- low-frequency hum;
- hiss or electric crackle;
- airflow or downwash;
- movement of grass, sand, leaves, clothing, or hair;
- visible turbulence;
- glow, sparks, corona, plasma, flame, exhaust, vapor, or smoke;
- heat sensation;
- pressure in ears, head, chest, or skin;
- ozone, burning, metallic, or unusual odor;
- lamp flicker or visible electrical disturbance;
- animal reaction.

## 10. Disappearance

When the row of lamps ended, the body left the accidentally illuminated volume. A weak contour remained visible for approximately one second and then disappeared into the dark sky. The disappearance is currently interpreted as a visibility-threshold event, not an observed physical dematerialization.

## 11. Witness attention

The witness kept visual attention on the object, except for a fraction-of-a-second glance toward friends while attempting to draw their attention to it.

## 12. Evidence boundary

- No known event photograph or video is available to the project.
- No sensor measurement is available.
- No independent witness record is currently available to the project.
- No recovered object or material is available.
- The exact site and exact day and month have not been reconstructed.
- Distance, height, dimensions, duration, and speed are remembered or later estimated rather than measured.
