# Evaluated Alternative Explanations

These notes describe tensions under the current witness record and later estimates. They do not prove that an explanation class is impossible. Distance, dimensions, height, speed, mass, wind, background sound, and detection thresholds are not calibrated.

## Perceptual interpretation, memory reconstruction, or scale error

These remain open. A retrospective single-witness record without event media or site calibration cannot eliminate misperception, memory change, or incorrect scale. A useful proposal should identify a specific visual scene, object, lighting condition, or cognitive mechanism and explain both the remembered features and later estimate changes.

## Misidentified conventional local object or platform

This remains open. The strongest version would identify a real platform available in or before summer 2004 and test its silhouette, lighting, speed, altitude, acoustics, airflow, and likely path against the record.

## Tether, cable, or suspension

Conditionally disfavored because no support structure was noticed and the remembered motion was coherent and nose-forward. The exact site, possible attachment geometry, nighttime visibility threshold, and object scale remain unknown, so the family is not eliminated.

## Kite, leaf, fabric, or wind-borne object

Conditionally disfavored if the corrected shore-to-sea wind and along-coast path are accurate. The witness remembers no obvious flutter, deformation, drift, or tether. A lightweight explanation remains viable if it can reproduce those remembered signatures under a concrete wind and viewing model.

## Balloon or aerostat

Open but under tension with the current dimension and motion estimates. Silence and low speed fit. Buoyant support depends on unknown thickness, internal volume, structure mass, wind, and active stabilization. A quantitative envelope and payload model is required before stronger conclusions.

## Conventional aerodynamic wing

Conditionally disfavored only under the current scale, area, speed, air-density, and substantial-mass assumptions. At approximately 2.7–4.2 m/s, modeled dynamic pressure is low. A smaller, lighter, faster, differently oriented, or mis-ranged object changes the conclusion materially.

## Multirotor, ducted fan, or distributed hidden fan

Conditionally disfavored for higher modeled masses because air-moving support predicts increasing flow, power, and acoustic signatures. The strength of this conflict depends on unknown mass, disk area, ducting, height, observer distance, background noise, and human detection thresholds. The irregular dark regions should not be treated as exact fan geometry.

## Jet, rocket, or turbine

Conditionally disfavored because the witness noticed no obvious sound, exhaust, heat, light, odor, or nearby material reaction. The negative observations are qualitative and cannot exclude a low-power, distant, shielded, or misidentified source without a concrete candidate model.

## Known electrohydrodynamic or ion-wind propulsion

Conditionally disfavored in currently demonstrated forms. Known implementations accelerate air, use high voltage, remain low-thrust, and commonly require exposed geometry or produce electrical and airflow effects. Quantitative scaling is required.

## Simple electrostatic lift

Conditionally disfavored as an isolated mechanism for a macroscopic body. Ordinary electric-field pressure at useful support levels implies high fields and possible corona, ozone, discharge, or environmental coupling. No electromagnetic measurements were made.

## Conventional magnetic levitation

Conditionally disfavored in ordinary infrastructure-dependent form because no compatible track, conductor, or external field source has been identified. The exact site has not been reconstructed, so infrastructure claims remain provisional.

## Acoustic levitation

Conditionally disfavored at the remembered scale because high support forces normally require intense acoustic fields. The witness noticed no obvious sound or pressure sensation, but no calibrated acoustic threshold exists.

## Radiation pressure

Conditionally disfavored at macroscopic support levels because simple models require extreme energy density and predict heating, optical, or radiation signatures. No relevant instruments were present.

## Very lightweight advanced platform

Open as a residual conventional family. Low mass reduces support force, power, sound, and downwash. It still needs a concrete construction, stability, wind-response, propulsion, and visibility model consistent with technology available by summer 2004 or an explicit reason to consider later technology.

## Review discipline

Every alternative should provide inputs, assumptions, expected signatures, sensitivity to uncertain geometry, and at least one discriminating observation or calculation. “Unidentified” is the present conclusion; it is not evidence for any specific alternative.
