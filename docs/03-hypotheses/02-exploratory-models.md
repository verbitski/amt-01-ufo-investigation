# Exploratory Project Models

This page explains why several author-developed models remain organized for analysis. Retention is not evidence that a model is true, physically realizable, or related to AMT-01.

## Selection criterion

An exploratory model should convert a reported feature into explicit requirements, dependencies, predictions, and failure conditions. It should not be promoted because a conventional explanation is incomplete.

## Force Skin

Force Skin is a mechanism-neutral distributed-support and control abstraction. It asks what force and moment distribution would be required to support and stabilize a body without assuming rotors, jets, or a new field. It does not identify the reaction channel and therefore is not itself a propulsion explanation.

## Dark-region or window hypotheses

The witness remembers several sharply bounded regions as completely black. Low reflectance, shadow, recess, opening, sensor window, field window, or memory reconstruction remain alternatives. Spectral selectivity is one testable hypothesis, not an observation.

## MEVE

MEVE explores the requirements for managing contact with a surrounding medium. AMT-01 did not report high-speed or underwater behavior, so this model is not required by the case. It belongs in the speculative sandbox.

## Q-MEVE

Q-MEVE is a Level-3 exclusion-boundary abstraction intended to expose questions about contact, pressure, and momentum. No known demonstrated field universally excludes neutral matter at macroscopic scale. Quantum terminology does not supply a mechanism.

## High-speed, transmedium, inertial, and warp extensions

These address claims from other reports or general thought experiments. AMT-01 itself contains no verified high-speed, underwater, high-acceleration, inertial-load, interstellar, or superluminal observation. They are outside the minimum model needed for this case.

Detailed model documents are isolated in [`docs/04-speculative-models/`](../04-speculative-models/README.md).
