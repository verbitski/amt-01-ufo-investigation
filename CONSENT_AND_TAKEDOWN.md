# Consent, Corrections, and Takedown

This policy explains what contributors consent to when they submit material for publication, how the canonical first-person record is controlled, and how to request correction or removal of project-controlled material.

## Private Witness Intake

The [AMT-01 witness-intake form](https://tally.so/r/5BgZMb) is the project's only private route for a new witness report and its primary route for access, copy, correction, consent-withdrawal, deletion, or another privacy request concerning an earlier private submission. Privacy requests may also be sent to [werbitsky@gmail.com](mailto:werbitsky@gmail.com). Tally hosts the form and processes and stores responses for the project. Project-level access to raw responses is limited to the project steward acting as the private-intake curator.

Submitting the form authorizes only the private storage and review described in [PRIVACY.md](PRIVACY.md). It does not authorize public quotation, publication, repository inclusion, dataset release, or licensing under the repository's public licenses. Name, pseudonym, and ordinary follow-up contact are optional for a new report; a response email is required for a privacy request. File uploads are optional, limited to five files of 10 MB each, and restricted to images, video, audio, PDF, and plain text.

Use a general location rather than a home address, precise private location, or coordinates that could identify or endanger someone. Do not use the form for emergencies, legally privileged material, credentials, classified information, identity documents, or another person's personal data.

The curator may review a submission, ask for follow-up if optional contact permission was provided, reject it, delete it, or propose a separately governed case record. No report is authenticated or accepted merely because it was submitted.

## Permission for Uploaded Material

By uploading a file for private review, you confirm that you created it or have authority to provide it for that limited purpose. You do not grant a public license merely by uploading it. The project must obtain separate, specific permission before publicly reproducing, distributing, adapting, quoting, or licensing a submission or file.

Any later publication agreement must identify the material covered, whether attribution or anonymity is requested, the approved redactions, the intended public location or dataset, and the applicable license or other permission. The project may decline publication even when permission is offered.

## Private-Submission Retention

Every raw Tally submission will be reviewed and manually deleted from the active response list no later than 12 months after submission and may be removed sooner when irrelevant, unsafe, duplicated, or withdrawn. This limit applies whether or not the report appears useful for later case-record work. It is a manual project process because automatic submission-retention control is not available on the current Tally plan.

Tally's [deletion documentation](https://tally.so/help/how-to-delete-and-recover-form-data) says deleted submissions remain in Trash for up to 90 days unless it is emptied earlier, and Tally's [GDPR information](https://tally.so/help/gdpr) says permanently deleted form data is cleared from backups within 90 days. A project deletion request can therefore remove a response from the active review list before all provider recovery copies complete their deletion lifecycle.

Before the 12-month limit expires, the submitter and curator may separately agree that identified material will be copied into a de-identified case record or retained source record for a stated purpose and period. The agreement must identify the material, access boundary, retention basis, publication status, and withdrawal or deletion consequences. Acceptance for further review alone does not create that agreement or authorize publication. The original raw Tally response remains subject to the 12-month active-list limit.

## Consent to Publish a Contribution

By intentionally submitting text, data, code, or media for inclusion in the public repository or website through a public contribution workflow, you confirm that:

1. you understand that the accepted material will be public;
2. you have the authority and permissions needed to submit and publish it;
3. the project may review, edit, reject, annotate, version, or remove it;
4. if accepted, it may be distributed under the license that applies to its repository path, as described in [LICENSE.md](LICENSE.md) and [RIGHTS_AND_ATTRIBUTION.md](RIGHTS_AND_ATTRIBUTION.md);
5. you have disclosed relevant authorship, sources, transformations, AI assistance, conflicts, and restrictions accurately; and
6. the contribution does not expose personal information or confidential material without the informed permission of the affected person.

Participation in a public discussion does not by itself make every message part of the repository or authorize the project to republish it as a witness account. A contribution becomes project material only through an intentional publication workflow and project acceptance.

A private Tally submission is not a public contribution and is excluded from this publication consent unless a separate publication agreement is later made.

## What the Public Licenses Mean

Project documentation, structured public data, the witness record, and rights-controlled project media are generally released under CC BY 4.0. Software and schemas are generally released under Apache-2.0. The exact path-based scope and exceptions are defined in [LICENSE.md](LICENSE.md).

CC BY 4.0 permits copying, redistribution, adaptation, and commercial use when its attribution and change-notice requirements are followed. Once material has been validly distributed under CC BY 4.0, that permission cannot be withdrawn from recipients who comply with the license. Apache-2.0 likewise permits continued use under its terms.

Removing a contribution from the current project does not cancel rights already granted, remove valid copies held by others, or guarantee deletion from Git history, forks, clones, mirrors, caches, release archives, or downstream adaptations.

## Media and Rights Declaration

Anyone proposing an image, video, audio file, model, dataset, or other media for publication must identify:

- its creator and the person authorized to license it;
- the source file and creation or supply date, when known;
- any third-party photograph, footage, font, model, texture, trademark, or other component;
- any AI or synthetic-media tool, provider, model, prompt, reference input, and material editing step, when known;
- the proposed license and any attribution or redistribution condition;
- whether recognizable people, private property, exact locations, voices, or sensitive metadata are present; and
- whether the witness or another affected person reviewed or approved the proposed use, when applicable.

Unknown information must be marked unknown. Do not claim ownership or consent that you cannot establish. The project may keep a file private, request more information, replace it, or decline publication.

Reconstructions must remain identified as reconstructions and must never be presented as photographs, video, measurements, or independent evidence from the reported event.

## Control of the First-Person Record

The canonical first-person witness record is curated under the witness-curator's control. Contributors may propose corrections, ask bounded questions, identify contradictions, or offer analysis, but they may not rewrite the canonical account in the witness's voice or attribute a conclusion to the witness without approval.

Corrections and later recollections are recorded transparently rather than silently replacing history. External adaptations permitted by CC BY 4.0 must identify their changes and must not be represented as an approved witness statement or project endorsement.

## Requesting a Correction or Takedown

For an ordinary factual correction that contains no sensitive information, open a GitHub issue and identify the relevant file, passage, source, and proposed correction.

For access, a copy, correction, consent withdrawal, deletion, or another request involving a private witness submission, use the privacy-request route in the [same witness-intake form](https://tally.so/r/5BgZMb) or email [werbitsky@gmail.com](mailto:werbitsky@gmail.com). For a privacy, consent, attribution, rights, safety, or sensitive-data concern involving already published project-controlled material, do not open a public issue; use the same private form, the privacy contact, or privately ask a moderator in the [project Discord server](https://discord.gg/JVvHf5cXhs) to route the concern. Provide only what is needed to locate and assess the material:

- the project-controlled URL, file path, asset, commit, or message;
- the nature of the concern;
- your relationship to the material or affected person;
- the requested correction, redaction, replacement, attribution, or removal; and
- supporting information that you are authorized to share.

The project may ask for reasonable verification before acting, particularly for ownership, identity, authorization, or consent claims. Sensitive supporting material will be limited to the people needed to review the request where practicable.

Requests will be handled as soon as practicable. The operational target is to respond or complete an initial assessment within 30 days, but this is not a guaranteed deadline and does not limit rights available under applicable law. Verification, safety, platform, backup, or legal complexity may require more time.

## Possible Outcomes

Depending on the evidence, rights, safety needs, license terms, and systems under project control, the project may:

- correct or annotate the record;
- remove personal data or hidden metadata;
- replace, restrict, or stop distributing an asset;
- update attribution, provenance, or license information;
- remove material from the current website, repository branch, or future release;
- preserve a minimal non-sensitive audit note explaining that a correction occurred; or
- decline a request and explain the reason privately when appropriate.

Urgent, credible risks may lead to temporary restriction while a review is underway. No outcome is guaranteed before the request is assessed.

## Limits of Takedown

The project can act only on systems and copies it controls. Git's distributed design and public licensing mean that earlier versions may continue to exist in commit history, forks, clones, mirrors, caches, archives, or downstream uses. The project cannot guarantee that those copies will disappear. A requester may also need to contact GitHub, Discord, the hosting provider, search engines, archive services, or downstream publishers directly under their own policies and applicable law.

This policy does not replace rights or remedies that may exist under applicable law.
