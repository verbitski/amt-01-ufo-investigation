## Witness record

The first-person record, later amendments, summaries, and structured public representation are released under CC BY 4.0 at the project owner's direction. This permits republication and adaptation, including commercial reuse, provided attribution, a source link, the license, and modification notice are supplied.

Reuse does not make an adaptation an approved witness statement. Modified versions must be identified as modified and must not imply endorsement by the witness or project.

## Project reconstruction media

The project owner supplied the active reconstructions for use in the website and has authorized open sharing with attribution and backlinks. Subject to the source-layer exceptions below, retained public reconstruction assets and their delivery derivatives are released under CC BY 4.0.

All such assets have the evidentiary role `reconstruction`. Their license does not authorize representing them as event photographs or independent evidence.

Detailed asset IDs, hashes, private-source identifiers, parent-child lineage, transformation parameters, and disclosure status are recorded in [`site/assets/provenance.json`](site/assets/provenance.json).

Every image distributed by the project contains the visible disclosure **RECONSTRUCTION / NOT EVENT MEDIA** baked into its pixels. The six lossless publication masters and eighteen responsive derivatives are built and checked by [`tools/build_public_media.sh`](tools/build_public_media.sh). Unlabeled masters remain outside the public repository and deployment tree.

CC BY 4.0 permits downstream adaptation. The project asks reusers to preserve the non-event-media disclosure, but does not add a no-removal restriction beyond the license. Reusers must identify modifications, provide the required attribution and license information, and must not imply endorsement.

## Source-layer limitation

The project licenses only rights it holds or controls. If an image contains a third-party stock photograph, font, model, texture, or other element governed by separate terms, that element remains subject to those terms. Discovery of an unrecorded third-party component triggers review, correction, replacement, or removal under the project's takedown process.

AI-assisted creation does not itself establish ownership of every input. Provider, model, prompt, reference, and editing details are recorded when known. Unknown provenance is stated as unknown rather than invented.

## External sources and quotations

The project license applies to original project summaries, annotations, and analysis. It does not apply to:

- linked government, academic, news, documentary, video, or archival works;
- third-party PDFs or media that are not committed to this repository;
- names, logos, and trademarks owned by others;
- quotations beyond the rights the project actually holds.

Those works retain their own copyright and license status.

## Superseded and archival assets

Unlabeled masters, provenance-preserving originals, and superseded versions are retained outside public deployment paths. Their presence in a local archive does not make them part of a public release.

## Corrections and takedown

Ordinary public corrections may use the guarded repository issue form. Rights, attribution, privacy, consent, safety, or provenance concerns should follow [CONSENT_AND_TAKEDOWN.md](CONSENT_AND_TAKEDOWN.md) and [PRIVACY.md](PRIVACY.md), using a private direct message to a Discord server moderator rather than a public issue. The project may stop distributing or replace a file, but an already granted CC BY 4.0 license cannot be revoked for recipients who comply with its terms.
